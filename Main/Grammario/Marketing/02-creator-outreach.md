# Phase 2 — Creator outreach

**Timeline:** Week 2–4
**Cost:** Low (free access + affiliate commissions)
**Goal:** Qualified traffic from trusted voices in the language learning space

> This is the highest ROI channel for niche SaaS. One mid-size YouTube creator in the German learning space doing a genuine review can send 500–2,000 highly qualified signups in a single week.

---

## YouTube language creators — mid-tier (10k–300k subs)

🟡 Medium effort

### Steps

1. Build a list of 30 YouTube channels across your 5 languages. Search "learn German," "Russian grammar," "learn Turkish" — focus on channels that do tool reviews or resources videos.
2. Watch 1–2 videos per creator. Find something specific you liked. Open your outreach email referencing it — this alone triples reply rate.
3. Offer: **free lifetime Pro access + 20–30% recurring affiliate commission** on any paid users they send. No upfront payment required from you.
4. Use [Gumroad](https://gumroad.com) or [Rewardful](https://rewardful.com) to set up affiliate tracking links. Takes ~1 hour to set up and is essential for this channel.
5. Follow up once, 7 days later, if no reply. Two emails max — don't pester.
6. If they say yes: send a pre-written script outline they can use, example analyses for their target language, and a quick demo video of the app.

### Target creators

| Creator | Language | Approx. subs | Notes |
|---------|----------|--------------|-------|
| German with Jenny | German | 500k+ | Beginner-intermediate, review-friendly |
| Easy German | German | 3M+ | More advanced, street interviews |
| Dreaming Spanish | Spanish | 2M+ | Immersion-focused, great fit |
| LanguageTransfer | Multiple | 200k+ | Free-tool ethos, may not monetize |
| Matt vs Japan | Japanese/polyglot | 200k+ | Reviews tools, analytical audience |
| Olly Richards (IWTYL) | Multiple | 200k+ | Has a blog + newsletter too |
| Luca Lampariello | Multiple | 300k+ | Polyglot, serious learner audience |

### Outreach tips

- Subject line: "Quick question about [specific video title]"
- Body: 3 sentences max before the ask
- Attach a 30-second screen recording of the app in action — most creators will watch it

## iTalki and Preply tutors

🟢 Low effort

### Steps

1. Search iTalki for top-rated tutors in German, Russian, Spanish, Italian, and Turkish. Look for those with 100+ hours taught and strong reviews.
2. Message 20 tutors introducing Grammario as a homework/feedback tool their students can use between sessions.
3. Offer an **educator account: unlimited use, free**, in exchange for recommending it to students.
4. The mechanic: tutor recommends → student subscribes → you gain a paying user who was already spending on language learning.
5. Build a simple one-page PDF explaining how tutors can integrate Grammario into lessons. Makes the ask feel professional.

## Newsletters and blogs

🟢 Low effort

### Steps

1. Find language learning newsletters on Substack and Beehiiv. Search "language learning," "polyglot," "learn German."
2. Offer a sponsored mention or free product feature — many small newsletters will feature interesting tools at no cost for the novelty.
3. Sites to contact for tool listings:
   - Fluent in 3 Months (benny@fluentin3months.com)
   - I Will Teach You a Language (iwtyl.com/contact)
   - Actual Fluency Podcast
4. Write a guest post for one of these blogs: *"Why grammar analysis beats grammar drilling for intermediate learners"* — include Grammario naturally at the end.
