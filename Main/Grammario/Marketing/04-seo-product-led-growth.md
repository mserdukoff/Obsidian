# Phase 4 — SEO and product-led growth

**Timeline:** Month 2+
**Cost:** Time investment; near-zero cash
**Goal:** Compounding organic traffic and users who bring in more users

---

## SEO — bottom-funnel keyword strategy

🔴 High effort (upfront), passive returns over time

### Steps

1. Install a free keyword tool: [Ubersuggest](https://neilpatel.com/ubersuggest) or connect [Google Search Console](https://search.google.com/search-console) to grammario.ai.
2. Target **bottom-funnel keywords** — people searching these are ready to use a tool:
   - "German grammar checker"
   - "Russian case analyzer"
   - "Turkish grammar help"
   - "Italian grammar tool"
   - "Spanish subjunctive checker"
3. Write one deep blog post per language you support. Example title: *"German accusative vs dative: why learners keep getting it wrong."* Solve a real pain, mention Grammario naturally at the end with a CTA.
4. Build **comparison pages**: "Grammario vs DeepL Write," "Grammario vs LanguageTool" — people searching comparisons are actively evaluating and ready to sign up.
5. Add structured FAQ schema to your landing page targeting long-tail questions (Google rewards this with featured snippets).
6. Submit grammario.ai to free tool directories for backlinks and discovery:
   - [AlternativeTo](https://alternativeto.net)
   - [Product Hunt](https://producthunt.com) (already in Phase 1)
   - [There's An AI For That](https://theresanaiforthat.com)
   - [Futurepedia](https://futurepedia.io)
   - [AI Tools Directory](https://aitoolsdirectory.com)

## Product-led growth — make users do the marketing

🟢 Low effort

### Steps

1. **Shareable analysis cards:** Let users export a grammar analysis as a visual image, lightly watermarked with "grammario.ai." Language learners love showing progress — they will post these on Reddit, Discord, and TikTok organically.

2. **Freemium funnel:** 10 free analyses/month, unlimited on paid ($9–12/mo). The free tier must be genuinely useful so users hit the limit and feel the value before converting.

3. **Referral program:** "Give a friend 1 month free, get 1 month free." Simple, no friction. Add it to the post-signup confirmation email and in-app dashboard.

4. **Email capture lead magnet:** "Free PDF: The 20 most common grammar errors in [language] and how to fix them." Collect emails, nurture with weekly grammar tips, convert to paid over time.

5. **Email drip sequence (suggested):**

| Day | Email |
|-----|-------|
| 1 | Welcome + how to get the most out of Grammario |
| 3 | 3 tips for using grammar analysis to actually improve |
| 7 | User story or testimonial showing real progress |
| 14 | Soft upgrade pitch — show what they're missing on free |
| 21 | "You've done X analyses this month" — usage recap + upgrade nudge |

   Use [Mailchimp](https://mailchimp.com) or [Resend](https://resend.com) — both have free tiers that cover early stage.

### Pricing structure to consider

| Tier | Price | Includes |
|------|-------|----------|
| Free | $0/mo | 10 analyses/month |
| Pro | $9–12/mo | Unlimited analyses, all 5 languages |
| Educator | $20–25/mo | Bulk seats, shareable student reports |

## Key principle for this phase

Every user who gets value should be able to share that value effortlessly. Shareable exports, referral links, and social-friendly outputs turn satisfied users into your distribution channel — and that's the compounding return that makes this phase worth investing in early.
